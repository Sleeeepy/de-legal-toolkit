# LfDI / BfDI directory — competent authority by operator residence

Required for Art. 77 DSGVO disclosure in Datenschutzerklärungen: the data subject has the right to lodge a complaint **with the competent supervisory authority** for the operator. Naming "die zuständige Datenschutzbehörde" generically is insufficient — the doc must name the specific authority.

Determining competence:
- **Public-sector operators**: the federal BfDI for federal bodies; the relevant LfDI for state/municipal bodies.
- **Private-sector operators**: the LfDI of the Bundesland where the operator's **Hauptniederlassung** (main establishment) is located. For solo proprietors, that's their residence/business address.
- **Multi-state operations**: One-Stop-Shop (Art. 56 DSGVO) for cross-border processing — the LfDI of the main establishment leads. For purely domestic single-state operators, the operator's home LfDI is competent.

## Bayern note

Bavaria is the only state with a **split** between public-sector and private-sector data-protection oversight. For a private business operating from Bavaria, the competent authority is the **BayLDA** (Ansbach), not the BayLfD. This is a common error in Datenschutzerklärungen — non-Bavarian doc generators often pick the public-sector authority.

## Cross-border / One-Stop-Shop

For operators with establishments in multiple EU states, the lead supervisory authority is the one in the Member State of the operator's **main establishment** (Art. 56 DSGVO). For a single-Member-State operator, the local Bundesland LfDI is competent and OSS does not apply.
